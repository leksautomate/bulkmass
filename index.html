What Is Bulkmass?

Bulkmass is a powerful bulk image generation tool built for creators, designers, marketers, and anyone who needs to produce large volumes of high-quality AI-generated images in a single session. Powered by Google's Imagen 3 model through the Whisk API, Bulkmass lets you turn dozens or even hundreds of text prompts into stunning visuals without the tedious process of generating them one at a time. Whether you are building a product catalog, creating social media content, designing game assets, or simply exploring creative ideas at scale, Bulkmass streamlines the entire workflow into a single dashboard where you can manage, monitor, and download everything in one place.

Traditional AI image generators force you to enter one prompt, wait for the result, save it, then repeat. That workflow breaks down the moment you need more than a handful of images. Bulkmass eliminates that bottleneck entirely. You paste your prompts, set your preferences, and hit start. The system queues every prompt, processes them through Google's image generation pipeline, and delivers the results directly to your browser in real time. There is no tab switching, no copy-pasting, and no lost outputs. Everything stays organized in a clean card grid with status tracking, image previews, and instant download options.

How It Works

Using Bulkmass is straightforward. The entire process can be broken down into a few simple steps that take you from authentication to a folder full of generated images in minutes.

Step 1: Authenticate with Your Google Cookie

Bulkmass uses your Google session cookie to communicate with the Whisk API on your behalf. To get started, visit aitestkitchen.withgoogle.com and sign in with your Google account. Once you are logged in, use a browser extension like EditThisCookie to export your session cookies as a JSON array, then paste the full JSON into the cookie input field in the sidebar. Click "Validate" and the system will validate your credentials against the Whisk API. If everything checks out, the status indicator turns green and you are ready to generate. Your cookie is stored locally in your browser so you do not need to paste it again on your next visit, though cookies do expire and you may need to refresh them periodically. The cookie expiration timer in the sidebar shows you exactly how much time you have left before re-authentication is needed.

Step 2: Configure Your Settings

Before you start generating, you can fine-tune a few settings to match your project requirements. The aspect ratio selector lets you choose between Square (1:1), Landscape (16:9), and Portrait (9:16) formats. This setting applies uniformly to every image in the batch, ensuring visual consistency across all your outputs. The count multiplier lets you generate multiple copies of each prompt, which is useful when you want variations of the same concept. For example, if you have 10 prompts and set the count to 3, Bulkmass will generate 30 images total, with 3 versions of each prompt.

Step 3: Add Your Style Prefix

One of the most useful features in Bulkmass is the style prefix. This is a text snippet that gets automatically prepended to every single prompt before it is sent to the AI model. By setting a style prefix like "cinematic lighting, 8k ultra detailed, oil painting style," every prompt in your batch inherits that same stylistic direction. This is incredibly powerful for maintaining visual consistency across a large set of images. Instead of manually writing the same style keywords into every prompt, you write it once in the style prefix box and it applies everywhere. The live preview below the prefix field shows you exactly what the first combined prompt will look like, so you can verify the formatting before committing to a full generation run.

Step 4: Enter Your Prompts

Prompts are the core input for image generation. You can add them in two ways. The first is by typing or pasting directly into the prompts textarea in the sidebar. Each line represents one prompt, so you can quickly paste a list of descriptions separated by line breaks. Lines starting with a hash symbol (#) are treated as comments and ignored, which is handy for organizing large prompt lists with section headers. The second method is file import. You can drag and drop a TXT, CSV, or JSON file onto the import zone, or click to browse. TXT files are parsed as one prompt per line. CSV files use the first column as the prompt text, automatically skipping any header row. JSON files can contain a flat array of strings or an array of objects with a "prompt" or "text" field. After import, the prompts appear in the textarea where you can edit them further before starting.

Step 5: Start Generating

Once your cookie is validated, your settings are configured, and your prompts are loaded, click the "Start" button at the bottom of the sidebar. Bulkmass begins processing your prompts one by one. Each prompt is sent to Google's Imagen 3 model through the Whisk API, and the resulting image is delivered directly to your browser and stored locally. You do not need to refresh the page or poll for updates. You will see each image appear in the card grid as it completes, with a live progress bar tracking overall completion at the top of the main content area. All images are stored in your browser's IndexedDB storage, so they persist even if you refresh the page.

Step 6: Review, Download, and Manage

As images finish generating, they appear as cards in a responsive grid. Click any image to open a full-size lightbox preview. Each card has buttons for downloading a single image or deleting it. When your batch is complete, you can download everything at once using the "Download ZIP" button, which packages all generated images into a compressed archive entirely in your browser. The sidebar provides additional actions: "Retry" resets any failed prompts so you can re-run them, and "Clear" wipes the entire workspace clean.

Key Features

Multi-User at Scale

Bulkmass is designed for concurrent use by thousands of users. Each user authenticates with their own Google cookie, and all processing state is managed locally in the browser. The server acts as a lightweight, stateless proxy that simply forwards requests to Google's Whisk API. This architecture means there is no shared state between users, no server-side job queue, and no risk of one user's activity affecting another. Whether 1 or 1000 users are generating images simultaneously, each user gets their own independent experience.

Browser-Native Storage

All generated images are stored in your browser's IndexedDB, a high-capacity local database. Your cookies, preferences, and generation queue are saved in localStorage. This means your data stays on your device, survives page refreshes, and requires zero server-side storage. You maintain full control over your data at all times.

Bulk Processing

Bulkmass handles batches of any size. The client-side queue processes prompts sequentially with intelligent rate limiting and exponential backoff on errors. File I/O is eliminated from the generation pipeline since images go directly from the API to your browser's storage.

Real-Time Progress Tracking

Every aspect of the generation process is visible in real time. The main progress bar shows overall batch completion. The sidebar displays a running count of completed and failed images. Each card in the grid shows its individual status with color-coded indicators.

Style Consistency with Prefix System

The style prefix feature ensures every prompt in your batch carries the same foundational style instructions. Whether you are generating product photos that all need the same lighting, illustrations with consistent art direction, or social media graphics with a uniform visual tone, the prefix system saves time and ensures consistency.

Smart Error Handling

Network issues and API rate limits are handled gracefully with exponential backoff. After 5 consecutive errors the queue pauses automatically. Failed prompts are clearly marked and can be retried with a single click. If your cookie expires mid-batch, the system detects the 401 error, pauses generation, and prompts you to re-authenticate.

Client-Side ZIP Download

Download all your generated images as a ZIP archive without any server involvement. The ZIP is created entirely in your browser using JSZip, reading directly from your local IndexedDB storage.

Who Is Bulkmass For?

Bulkmass is built for anyone who needs AI-generated images in quantity. Content creators use it to produce batches of social media graphics, blog illustrations, and thumbnail images in a single session. E-commerce sellers use it to generate product mockups, lifestyle imagery, and catalog visuals across entire product lines. Game developers and indie studios use it for concept art exploration, generating dozens of environment, character, or item variations from a single prompt list. Marketers use it to produce A/B test assets, ad creatives, and campaign imagery at a pace that would be impossible with one-at-a-time generation tools.

If you have ever found yourself copying the same prompt into an AI image generator over and over, tweaking one word at a time and saving each result manually, Bulkmass is the tool that automates that entire workflow and gives you back your time.

Getting Started

To start using Bulkmass, you need a Google account with access to AI Test Kitchen at aitestkitchen.withgoogle.com. Sign in, export your session cookies using a browser extension, and paste them into the cookie input field in the sidebar. Once validated, enter your prompts either manually or by importing a file, optionally set a style prefix for consistency, choose your aspect ratio, and click "Start." Your images will begin appearing in the card grid within seconds, and you can download them individually or as a ZIP archive when the batch is complete.
